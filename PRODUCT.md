# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Nightlife enthusiasts, event-goers, and venue owners (primarily focused on electronic music, techno, house, etc. in Romania and globally). They use NOCTVM to discover events, book tickets (Pocket), share ephemeral moments (Stories), and navigate the nightlife scene.

## Product Purpose

NOCTVM is the "living memory of the night" — a premium nightlife discovery and social platform. It connects people with events, venues, and each other through a highly polished, nocturnal interface. Success looks like: users discovering events they attend, venues gaining visibility, and the social feed becoming the default way to share nightlife moments.

## Brand Personality

Premium, exclusive, nocturnal, highly polished. The brand evokes the energy of high-end nightlife. Three words: **Nocturnal**, **Premium**, **Electric**.

## Anti-references

- Generic gradient-heavy event apps (Eventbrite clones, neon-on-dark clichés)
- Social feeds that look like Instagram/TikTok knockoffs
- Light mode interfaces (NOCTVM is strictly dark)
- System default fonts (Inter, Roboto, Arial)
- Card-heavy dashboards with no spatial rhythm
- Side-stripe borders on cards or list items
- "Elevate", "Discover", "Unlock" AI copy

## Design Principles

1. **Unslop the UI**: Eliminate generic AI design choices. No gradient text for metrics, no rounded rectangles with thick colored borders on one side, no pure gray text on colored backgrounds.
2. **Spatial Rhythm**: Avoid monotonous card grids. Use asymmetric layouts and intentional spacing to create visual hierarchy.
3. **Impeccable Typography**: Never use system default fonts. Maintain strict hierarchy from massive Hero typography down to Micro monospaced tags.
4. **Contextual Glass**: Glass panels are the foundational surface, coming in strict variants (default, modal, subtle, noise). Do not invent new glass formulas.
5. **Progressive Disclosure**: Use Popovers, Dropdowns, and Collapsibles to keep the interface clean while retaining power-user functionality.
6. **Practice what you preach**: The app itself should feel like the premium nightlife experience it promotes.
7. **Show, don't tell**: Let the visual design communicate exclusivity without relying on explanatory copy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA target
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected — glass effects flatten under reduced motion
- `prefers-contrast: more` flattens glass for legibility
- Colorblind-safe accent usage (violet + emerald + gold provide multiple visual channels)
